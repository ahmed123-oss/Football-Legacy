History of Al Ahly FC

The Foundation of Al Ahly SC (1907) Al Ahly Sporting Club was founded on April 24, 1907, by a group of prominent Egyptian nationalists led by Omar Lotfi Bey.
The club's main goal was to provide a space for young Egyptian patriots to meet and practice sports during the nationalist movement against British colonial rule. The name "Al Ahly" translates to "The National," symbolizing its strong connection to the Egyptian people.
First Chairman:
The club’s first president was an Englishman, Mitchel Ince, who was chosen for his expertise in sports administration. However, Egyptian presidents, starting with Abdel Khalek Tharwat, took over leadership in the years that followed. The Early Years of Sports (1911–1922) 1911: Al Ahly established its first football team, marking the beginning of its journey in Egyptian football. 1922: Al Ahly became a founding member of the Egyptian Football Association (EFA), showcasing its growing importance in national sports. 1920s: Dominating Local Competitions Al Ahly started participating in local tournaments, particularly the Cairo League and the Egyptian Cup, which were the top competitions at the time. The club became synonymous with success, consistently winning titles and establishing a fierce rivalry with Zamalek SC.
1940s: The Birth of a Giant The 1940s were a golden era for Al Ahly. The club began to dominate the Cairo League and the Egyptian Cup, solidifying its position as the premier club in Egypt. By the end of this decade, Al Ahly had established a winning mentality and a large fanbase.
1950s and 1960s: Regional Recognition Al Ahly’s influence extended beyond Egypt during these decades:
The club participated in international friendlies, gaining regional and continental recognition. The Egyptian national team, composed largely of Al Ahly players, won several regional tournaments. In 1957, Al Ahly helped lay the foundation for the Confederation of African Football (CAF). 1970s: Introduction of the African Competitions The 1970s marked a turning point for African football:
Al Ahly began competing in CAF tournaments, particularly the CAF Champions League. In 1976, Mahmoud El Khatib joined the club. He would later become one of the greatest players in African history and a future president of Al Ahly. 1980s: Continental Dominance Begins The 1980s were groundbreaking for Al Ahly:
The club won its first CAF Champions League in 1982, defeating Ghana's Asante Kotoko. This era saw Al Ahly dominate both locally and continentally, earning the nickname “The Club of the Century” due to its unparalleled success. 1990s: Becoming the Club of the Century In 2000, CAF officially named Al Ahly “Africa's Club of the Century” for its record-breaking achievements. The 1990s saw Al Ahly continue to dominate Egyptian and African football, despite fierce competition from clubs like Zamalek and Tunisia's Esperance. 2000s: The Golden Generation The 2000s were an extraordinary period for Al Ahly:
Under the leadership of legendary coach Manuel Jose, the club won the CAF Champions League in 2001, 2005, 2006, and 2008. Al Ahly set an African record by going 55 games unbeaten in all competitions. In 2006, Al Ahly finished third in the FIFA Club World Cup, becoming the first African club to achieve this milestone. 2010s: Consistency and Records Al Ahly continued to dominate both local and continental competitions, winning the CAF Champions League in 2012 and 2013. Despite political turmoil in Egypt, Al Ahly maintained its excellence, securing multiple Egyptian Premier League and Cup titles. 2020s: Modern-Day Excellence Al Ahly has cemented its position as the most successful club in African history:
In 2020, Al Ahly won its 9th CAF Champions League title, defeating Zamalek in the first-ever all-Egyptian final. The club won back-to-back Champions League titles in 2021 and 2022, taking its tally to 11 titles. In 2021, Al Ahly achieved another historic feat by winning the CAF Super Cup and finishing third in the FIFA Club World Cup. Trophies and Honors As of 2025, Al Ahly’s honors include:
44 Egyptian Premier League titles (a record). 38 Egyptian Cup titles (a record). 12 CAF Champions League titles (the most by any African club). Multiple CAF Super Cups, CAF Confederation Cups, and international recognitions. Legacy Al Ahly SC remains a symbol of Egyptian pride and African excellence. Its story is not just about football but about resilience, unity, and passion, making it a club beloved by millions worldwide.
