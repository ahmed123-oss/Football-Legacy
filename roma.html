History of AS Roma

AS Roma: A Comprehensive History from Its Foundation to 2025 Foundation and Early Years (1927–1930s) AS Roma, or Associazione Sportiva Roma, was founded on July 22, 1927, through the merger of three clubs: Roman FC, SS Alba-Audace, and Fortitudo-Pro Roma SGS. The consolidation was aimed at creating a team capable of challenging the dominance of northern Italian clubs in Serie A. The club adopted the colors of the city of Rome, red and yellow, symbolizing Roman imperial dignity and the city’s rich history. Roma initially played its matches at the Motovelodromo Appio before moving to the Campo Testaccio in 1929, which became a fortress for the club and an iconic venue for its passionate fans. During this period, Roma developed a competitive squad but did not achieve significant success, as Serie A was dominated by teams like Juventus and Bologna. The First Scudetto (1941–1942) Roma achieved its first major triumph during the 1941–42 season, winning the Serie A title. Under coach Alfred Schaffer and led by star player Amedeo Amadei, nicknamed "Il Fornaretto" (The Little Baker), Roma became the first southern club to win the Scudetto. This victory was a monumental achievement, breaking the northern stranglehold on Italian football. The post-war years saw Roma struggle to maintain its position at the top. Financial difficulties and inconsistent performances kept the club from challenging for titles, and in 1951, Roma was relegated to Serie B. However, the club quickly bounced back, earning promotion to Serie A in the following season. Revival and Domestic Cup Success (1960s–1980s) The 1960s marked a period of stability and moderate success for Roma. The club won its first Coppa Italia in 1964, defeating Torino in the final. The decade also saw the emergence of legendary players like Giacomo Losi, who became a symbol of loyalty and determination. The 1980s were a golden era for Roma. Under the guidance of coach Nils Liedholm, Roma won its second Scudetto during the 1982–83 season, led by captain Agostino Di Bartolomei and Brazilian superstar Falcão. The team also reached the final of the European Cup (now the Champions League) in 1984 but lost to Liverpool in a dramatic penalty shootout at the Stadio Olimpico. Roma added multiple Coppa Italia titles to its collection during this period, solidifying its status as one of Italy’s top clubs. The Totti Era: A Club Legend (1990s–2010s) The 1990s and 2000s were defined by the emergence of Francesco Totti, widely regarded as one of the greatest players in football history. Totti, a Roman born and bred, made his debut for Roma in 1993 and became the face of the club for more than two decades. Known as "Il Capitano" (The Captain), Totti’s skill, vision, and loyalty endeared him to fans worldwide. Under coach Fabio Capello, Roma won its third Scudetto during the 2000–01 season, with Totti, Gabriel Batistuta, and Vincenzo Montella forming a lethal attacking trio. This title remains one of the most cherished moments in the club’s history. Throughout the 2000s and 2010s, Roma consistently competed at the top of Serie A but often fell short of winning the league, finishing as runners-up several times. The club continued to achieve success in domestic cup competitions, winning multiple Coppa Italia titles and the Supercoppa Italiana. European Ambitions and Modern Challenges (2010s–2020s) The 2010s saw Roma focus on improving its European performances. In the 2017–18 UEFA Champions League, Roma achieved one of the greatest comebacks in football history, overturning a 4–1 first-leg deficit against Barcelona to win 3–0 at the Stadio Olimpico and advance to the semifinals. This victory highlighted Roma’s resilience and ability to compete with Europe’s elite. However, the club faced challenges in maintaining consistency, both domestically and in Europe. Frequent changes in management and financial constraints hindered Roma’s ability to challenge for major titles. Despite these obstacles, the club remained a competitive force in Italian football. The Mourinho Era and the Europa Conference League Triumph (2021–2025) In 2021, Roma appointed José Mourinho as head coach, signaling a new era of ambition and determination. Under Mourinho’s leadership, Roma won the inaugural UEFA Europa Conference League in 2022, defeating Feyenoord in the final. This victory was Roma’s first European trophy and a significant milestone in the club’s history. Building on this success, Roma reached the final of the Europa League in 2023 but narrowly lost to Sevilla. Despite the setback, Mourinho’s tenure revitalized the club, with players like Paulo Dybala, Tammy Abraham, and Lorenzo Pellegrini playing pivotal roles. Looking Ahead to 2025 As of 2025, AS Roma continues to be one of the most passionate and storied clubs in Italian football. The club’s fanbase, known as the Giallorossi, remains fiercely loyal, creating an electrifying atmosphere at the Stadio Olimpico. Roma is focused on building a squad capable of challenging for the Serie A title and achieving sustained success in European competitions. With a rich history, a strong identity rooted in the city of Rome, and an ambitious vision for the future, AS Roma embodies the spirit of resilience and passion that defines Italian football. The club looks to honor its legacy while striving for new heights in the years to come.
