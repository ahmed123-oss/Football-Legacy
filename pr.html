History of Pyramids FC

Pyramids FC: A Comprehensive History from Its Foundation to 2025 Early Years (2008–2015) Pyramids FC, originally known as Al Assiouty Sport, was founded in 2008 in the Egyptian city of Assiut. The club started as a modest project, competing in the lower tiers of Egyptian football. In its early years, the club struggled to gain attention and recognition, often overshadowed by the more prominent clubs in the country. However, Al Assiouty showed ambition and a desire to progress through Egypt's football ranks. The club’s breakthrough came during the 2014–2015 season, when they achieved promotion to the Egyptian Premier League for the first time in its history. Unfortunately, the team faced a tough challenge in the top flight, and despite showing promise, they were relegated back to the second division in the same season. This setback, however, was not the end of the club's aspirations. Return to the Premier League and Transformation (2015–2018) After a brief stint in the lower divisions, Al Assiouty made a strong comeback to the Egyptian Premier League in the 2017–2018 season. The club’s performances began to improve, and they showed their potential as a competitive team, setting the stage for a significant change in the club's fortunes. However, the most dramatic shift came in 2018. Acquisition by Turki Al-Sheikh (2018) In 2018, a new chapter began for the club when Saudi businessman and sports investor Turki Al-Sheikh acquired the majority stake in the club. Al-Sheikh, who had previously been involved in Egyptian football through his association with the Egyptian Football Association, brought a new level of investment and ambition to the club. The first and most obvious change was the rebranding of the club, from Al Assiouty Sport to Pyramids FC. The name change reflected the club's new identity and ambitions. Under Al-Sheikh’s ownership, Pyramids FC underwent a major overhaul. Significant investments were made in the team’s infrastructure, including a new stadium in Cairo and improved training facilities. The club’s roster was also significantly upgraded with high-profile signings, attracting international players and coaches to help elevate the team’s competitive level. This included notable players from Europe and South America, as well as seasoned Egyptian players. Pyramids FC’s Rise in Egyptian Football (2018–2020) Pyramids FC’s rise was swift. The club finished in third place in the 2018–2019 Egyptian Premier League, securing a spot in continental competitions for the first time in its history. The team also made an impact in the CAF Confederation Cup, reaching the final in 2020, which helped solidify their position as a major force in African football. Despite not winning the league during these early years, Pyramids FC quickly established itself as a title contender, challenging the dominance of the traditional giants of Egyptian football, Al Ahly and Zamalek. The team's performances on the domestic front, as well as its progress in continental tournaments, caught the attention of football fans across Africa. Turki Al-Sheikh’s Departure and Continued Development (2019–2021) In 2019, Al-Sheikh faced increasing pressure and eventually stepped down from his role at the club, leading to a change in ownership. The club was acquired by a consortium of Emirati investors, but the ambition and investment in the club remained high. Under this new ownership, Pyramids FC continued to improve both on and off the field. The club’s roster remained filled with top talent, and the management remained focused on developing a competitive squad capable of winning domestic and continental titles. Pyramids FC in African Competitions (2021–2023) Pyramids FC's success continued in continental competitions, particularly in the CAF Confederation Cup and CAF Champions League. In the 2021 season, Pyramids made an impact in the CAF Champions League, progressing to the knockout stages, although they fell short of winning the prestigious African title. The club's success in the Confederation Cup continued, and they became a regular fixture in the tournament’s latter stages, firmly establishing their presence on the African football scene. At the domestic level, Pyramids FC continued to challenge for the Egyptian Premier League title but had to contend with the might of Al Ahly and Zamalek. While they did not manage to secure the league title during these years, they remained one of the most competitive teams in Egypt, consistently finishing in the upper part of the table. Pyramids FC’s Identity and Strategy (2023–2025) As of 2023 and into 2025, Pyramids FC has firmly established itself as one of the top clubs in Egypt and Africa. The club’s strategy has been clear: to build a modern, competitive football team capable of challenging the traditional powers in both domestic and international competitions. Pyramids FC’s ownership continues to invest in the development of the squad, with a focus on young talent and experienced internationals. The club has focused on modernizing its approach to football, with advanced training facilities and a focus on sports science and nutrition. Pyramids FC has also invested in building a global fan base, leveraging its connections and partnerships around the world. Looking Forward As of 2025, Pyramids FC continues to challenge for both the Egyptian Premier League and CAF Champions League titles. The club has positioned itself as a major player in Egyptian football, and its ambitions are clear: to secure the domestic league title and establish itself as a dominant force in African football. With significant investments in player recruitment and facilities, Pyramids FC is poised for further success in the years to come. The journey of Pyramids FC is one of rapid transformation, from a lower-tier club to one of Egypt’s most ambitious and well-resourced teams, with the goal of competing at the highest levels of African football.
